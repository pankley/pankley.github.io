A nontargeted LC-HRMS metabolomics study characterizing the sublethal effects
of tire-derived antiozonants in the rainbow trout gill cell line RTgill-W1.
The work contrasts parent p-phenylenediamines (PPDs) with their environmentally
relevant quinone transformation products (PPD-Qs), with particular focus on
6PPD and the acutely toxic 6PPD-quinone.

**Highlights**

- Nontargeted LC-HRMS workflow applied to a fish gill cell model relevant
  to environmental exposure routes
- Mechanistic interpretation spanning oxidative stress, the integrated
  stress response, and amino acid depletion patterns
- Direct comparison of parent compounds and transformation products to
  inform structure–activity relationships
- Supports the RTgill-W1 line as a tractable new approach methodology (NAM)
  for tire-chemical hazard assessment

Manuscript in peer review.
