I am an environmental toxicologist specializing in omics-driven mechanistic toxicology. With over seven years of experience, I integrate transcriptomics, nontargeted metabolomics, amplicon sequencing, bioinformatics, and high-resolution mass spectrometry to characterize chemical hazards across diverse toxicological and environmental contexts. My research has spanned multiple contaminant classes — including per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS), tire antiozonants, antimicrobials, and pharmaceuticals — encompassing both effects characterization and biotransformation. I bring expertise in toxicity testing, analytical chemistry, and bioinformatics, with hands-on experience designing and executing in vitro, in vivo, and in situ studies, enabling me to tackle complex, multidisciplinary problems.
